A Common Lisp dialect
that fits in your head.

glu is a handful of macros and one rule: the subject is implicit. Drop glu.lisp into any CL file and you have it. Same dispatch, half the characters.

Plain Common Lisp

(defstruct num
  "Welford summary."
  (n 0) (mu 0) (m2 0))

(defmethod add ((s num) v)
  (incf (slot-value s 'n))
  (let ((d (- v (slot-value s 'mu))))
    (incf (slot-value s 'mu)
          (/ d (slot-value s 'n)))
    (incf (slot-value s 'm2)
          (* d (- v (slot-value s 'mu))))))

(defmethod mid ((s num))
  (slot-value s 'mu))

(defmethod spread ((s num))
  (if (< (slot-value s 'n) 2) 0
      (sqrt (/ (slot-value s 'm2)
               (1- (slot-value s 'n))))))

glu

(glu num "Welford summary."
    ((n 0) (mu 0) (m2 0))

  (add (v)
    (incf $n)
    (let ((d (- v $mu)))
      (incf $mu (/ d $n))
      (incf $m2 (* d (- v $mu)))))

  (mid    () $mu)
  (spread () (if (< $n 2) 0
               (sqrt (/ $m2 (1- $n))))))

One glu form replaces a defstruct plus three defmethods. Every (slot-value s 'X) shrinks to $X. Read the macros or see a bigger example.
